1. SITE OVERVIEW
The Meridian North Calibration Site is a primary meteorological and solar research facility operated by the Department of Energy Research within the Federative Republic of Althea. Situated in the Althean northern-tier, the site serves as a benchmarking station for high-latitude solar resource assessment and instrument verification.

2. TECHNICAL DEFINITION: GLOBAL HORIZONTAL IRRADIANCE (GHI)
Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) is defined as the total shortwave radiation received from above by a surface horizontal to the ground. This measurement quantifies atmospheric sun energy and is comprised of both Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI) and Diffuse Horizontal Irradiance (DHI). GHI is the critical metric used by the Department to determine the available solar resource for stationary photovoltaic installations and atmospheric energy balance models.

3. THEORETICAL MAXIMUM SCENARIO
For a northern-tier profile such as that of Meridian North, the theoretical maximum irradiance scenario is calculated based on solar geometry, latitude, and clear-sky assumptions. During the winter-to-spring transition, the expected profile typically follows a standard diurnal bell curve, with a rapid increase toward solar noon and a symmetrical decline toward sunset. However, localized factors can influence the actualized peak versus the potential clear-sky maximum.

5. OBSERVATIONAL SUMMARY
Analysis of the recorded GHI time series throughout this three-day window reveals a trend of stagnant or flat peak irradiance readings. Unlike the sharp apex expected in a standard solar profile, the data points reached a plateau and remained steady at approximately 700 Watts per squared meter (W/m²).

Technical review confirms that this truncation and lack of variance at peak solar noon was caused by atmospheric haze calibration. During this specific window, sensors were adjusted to account for persistent particulate matter in the upper atmosphere, resulting in the stabilization of readings at the observed 700 W/m² threshold despite variations in actual luminous intensity. This calibration ensures that the instruments maintain a baseline for atmospheric energy absorption under specific haze conditions.

6. DATA VALIDATION
Specific data points from the recording equipment confirm that consistent peak levels were maintained around 700 Watts per squared meter for the duration of the 72-hour observation window. This consistency validates the calibration settings applied to the Meridian North sensor array for the February 2022 reporting cycle.
