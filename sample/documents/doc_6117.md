1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE
This document serves as the formal technical manual update and operational audit for the Site Station facility. It establishes the technical specifications for onsite hardware and outlines the historical and administrative parameters governing site operations.

2. HISTORICAL CONTEXT: THE HIGH PLAINS MERIDIAN
The site is centered upon the High Plains Meridian, a geographic marker of significant historical importance. The meridian was discovered during the initial topographical surveys of the Althean interior, identifying a unique longitudinal alignment used for early maritime and overland navigation. The current facility was constructed specifically to preserve this historical point of reference while integrating modern observational instruments.

3. SOLAR NOON CALCULATION METHDOLOGY
Historically, the Site Station has utilized a specific descriptive methodology for determining solar noon. This process involves the following sequence:
- Shadow Length Minimization: Utilizing the High Plains Meridian marker as a local gnomon to observe the shortest shadow cast.
- Apparent Solar Time Assessment: Tracking the sun's transit across the local meridian using manual optical alignment.
- Equation of Time Adjustment: Applying a standardized temporal correction to convert Local Apparent Time (LAT) to the civil time standard used by the Federative Republic of Althea.
This methodology has provided the baseline for all historical solar data recorded at this location.

4. DATA LOGGER SPECIFICATIONS
As part of the current operational audit, the following specifications for the local data logger hardware are verified:

4.1 Storage Capacity
The onsite data logger is equipped with an internal solid-state memory module with a total capacity of 64GB. Under standard sampling rates, this allows for the local retention of approximately 18 months of raw observational data before archival is required.

4.2 Backup Cycle and Protocol
To ensure data integrity, the system follows a mandated backup frequency:
- Cycle Frequency: Automated backups are performed every 24 hours at 00:00 local time.
- Protocol: Data is mirrored from the primary logger partition to an onsite redundant storage array via an encrypted serial bus. Upon completion of the backup, a checksum is generated to verify packet integrity against the primary record.

5. ADMINISTRATIVE SCHEDULING AND LOGISTICS
Site supervisors must account for local administrative regulations regarding staffing and site accessibility.

5.1 Local Holiday Observance
Please be advised that February 8 is a designated local holiday within the Federative Republic of Althea. On this date, standard maintenance cycles are suspended. The site operates in autonomous mode, and physical access is restricted to emergency protocols only.
