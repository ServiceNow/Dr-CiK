1. OVERVIEW AND MODELING SCOPE
This technical report presents the results of a solar potential analysis based on a modeled clear-sky schedule for the Arid Heights Research Annex. The data set provides projected energy density values across three consecutive 24-hour cycles, focusing on identifying peak solar flux and defining operational windows. As per the technical scope, this source focuses exclusively on specific solar flux measurements and projections. It does not include geographical location data, seasonal weather patterns, long-term grid impact assessments, or financial implications.

2. ANALYSIS OF DIURNAL POTENTIAL
The model identifies peak solar flux corresponding to the highest solar elevation, contrasting significantly with established night periods. A consistent finding throughout the projection is that the measured potential is zero during pre-dawn hours from 00:00 to 06:00. These potential measurements of zero are recorded across three monitored dates due to the lack of direct solar exposure in the modeled clear-sky profile.

Similarly, the measured potential is zero during post-sunset intervals from 18:00 to 21:00. This absence of energy density occurs uniformly across the three monitored dates, reflecting the definitive lack of direct solar exposure following the decline of the solar disk.
