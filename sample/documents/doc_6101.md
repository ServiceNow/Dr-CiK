Technical Report: Standardization of Downward Solar Flux Measurement Protocols

To maintain consistency across the High Plains Meridian and the Arid Heights Research Annex, the following standards for solar flux measurement are established. The geometric arrangement of sensors must be strictly calibrated to ensure that all captured data reflects the total power density on a perfectly level, non-tilted area. Because these protocols restrict measurement exclusively to a horizontal plane, a formal categorization for the resulting data is required for organizational clarity. Consequently, the specific integrated value of downward solar flux utilized in meteorological modeling is officially assigned the standardized scientific designation of Global Horizontal Irradiance. These nomenclature and geometric requirements ensure uniform data integration across the Federative Republic of Althea.
