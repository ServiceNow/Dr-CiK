SUBJECT: Obsidian Valley Solar Farm: Site Irradiance and Shadowing Analysis Report

1. SITE BACKGROUND
The Obsidian Valley Solar Farm is a strategic energy asset located within the Federative Republic of Althea. Situated in the deep geological depression of the Obsidian Valley, the site is characterized by high-rimmed basaltic ridges. While the region is known for clear atmospheric conditions, the unique topography of the valley significantly influences the operational window for solar harvesting. This report evaluates the specific irradiance patterns resulting from the surrounding terrain.

2. TECHNICAL DEFINITION: GLOBAL HORIZONTAL IRRADIANCE (GHI)
For the purposes of this analysis, Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) is defined as the total shortwave radiation received from above by a surface horizontal to the ground. This measurement includes both Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI) and Diffuse Horizontal Irradiance (DHI) collected by sensors mounted on horizontal planes. GHI is the definitive metric for assessing the potential solar resource available at the site before accounting for module tilt or tracking.

3. MICRO-CLIMATE IRRADIANCE SCENARIO
The micro-environment of Obsidian Valley presents a localized irradiance scenario distinct from the broader regional climate. Under cloudless sky conditions—which are the standard for this assessment—the site does not receive the theoretical maximum solar insolation typical of its latitude. Instead, the high-altitude topography creates a restricted solar window. Even during perfectly clear meteorological conditions, the physical height of the valley walls obstructs the solar disc during the morning and afternoon hours.

5. OBSERVATIONAL DATA ANALYSIS
Detailed analysis of the sensors on site confirms a significant restriction in the daily irradiance profile. Observation data confirms that non-zero irradiance levels occur exclusively between 11:00:00 and 13:00:00. Outside of this two-hour window, the GHI remains at zero. 

This phenomenon is attributed entirely to valley shadowing. The sun only clears the eastern ridge at approximately 11:00:00, and the solar disc disappears behind the western ridge by 13:00:00. This report serves as a record of these site-specific constraints; it does not provide generation forecasting or broader policy context. This data is strictly for the assessment of the physical site characteristics and recorded levels for the specified dates.
