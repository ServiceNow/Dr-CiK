Solar Asset Management Quality Assurance Team
Operational Audit Report: Technical Performance Audit
Reference ID: SAM-QA-2024-GHI-882

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This technical performance audit focuses strictly on the failure of the GHI Predictive Model within the specified evaluation period. The audit identifies localized atmospheric factors affecting panel cooling and examines the technical failure of the model’s algorithmic output. This report does not contain financial forecasts or mitigation strategies.

2. MODEL OVERVIEW AND PERFORMANCE DISCREPANCIES
The GHI Predictive Model was designed to estimate Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) by leveraging specific atmospheric inputs. However, internal testing has confirmed the generation of incorrect solar irradiance values. The primary technical failure stems from the model’s architecture, which uses only wind speed as a predictive variable for GHI, failing to integrate additional irradiance-related sensors or multi-spectral data points.

3. ATMOSPHERIC ANALYSIS: WIND PATTERNS AND PANEL COOLING
Analysis of the regional wind patterns indicates a significant influence on the convection rates and subsequent solar panel cooling within the site cluster. Regional wind patterns act as a primary cooling mechanism, maintaining module temperatures within operational bounds. 

There is a confirmed causal link between wind speed and GHI efficiency across the arrays. Higher wind speeds facilitate accelerated heat dissipation from the photovoltaic cells; as thermal resistance decreases, the conversion efficiency of the GHI captured by the cells increases proportionally. The predictive model fails to properly weight this relationship, leading to skewed efficiency projections when wind speeds fluctuate.

4. DATA INTEGRITY AND TEMPORAL CORRELATION
A high-resolution audit of the system logs revealed a critical synchronization error in the dataset. The logs contain timestamps matching the original audit timeframe, which were intended to be paired with GHI sensor readings. However, these timestamps are paired with irrelevant wind velocity data that does not correspond to the actual atmospheric conditions recorded at the localized sensors during those moments.

6. AUDIT CONCLUSION
The audit confirms that the GHI Predictive Model’s reliance on a singular input—wind speed—is insufficient for calculating solar irradiance. While the causal link between wind speed and panel cooling is verified as a significant factor in localized GHI efficiency, the model’s failure to differentiate between irrelevant velocity data and actual environmental inputs has resulted in the dissemination of incorrect irradiance values. This report remains confined to the aforementioned technical failures and atmospheric cooling dynamics.
