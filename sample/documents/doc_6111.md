Date: Q3 Operating Cycle

1. SYSTEM STORAGE CAPACITY
The Althea electrical grid maintains a distributed storage infrastructure designed for frequency regulation and load shifting. The current aggregate storage capacity is rated for a nameplate energy volume of 1,250 MWh, utilizing a mix of lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) stationary arrays. This capacity is strategically partitioned to ensure a minimum spinning reserve of 15% at all times, with the remaining volume available for daily peak shaving and localized grid stability across the authority’s jurisdiction.

2. GHI-TRIGGERED CONSERVATION PROTOCOLS
Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) serves as the primary metric for automated energy conservation triggers. The Grid Dispatch Center monitors real-time GHI sensors located at primary solar collection points. This protocol follows a tiered response based on irradiance levels:

- Phase I (Moderate Decline): If GHI drops below 350 W/m² during peak solar hours for a sustained period of 15 minutes, the system initiates 'Soft Conservation,' reducing non-essential municipal lighting loads.
- Phase II (Critical Decline): If GHI levels fall below 150 W/m² due to atmospheric conditions or cloud cover, the system triggers 'High-Efficiency Mode.' This forces a reduction in industrial HVAC cycles and engages residential demand-response protocols to preserve battery state-of-charge (SoC).
- Phase III (Grid Protective State): Persistent GHI readings below 50 W/m² during daylight operational cycles mandate the suspension of all non-critical heavy equipment usage across the grid to prevent rapid storage depletion.

3. HIGH PLAINS MERIDIAN: GRID-TIE POWER LIMITS
The High Plains Meridian (HPM) sub-sector is governed by specific thermal and transformer capacity limits to prevent substation degradation. The power limits for the HPM interconnection points are established as follows:

- Maximum Sustained Import: 450 MW
- Maximum Sustained Export: 325 MW
- Peak Transient Limit (10-minute ceiling): 500 MW
- Reactive Power Support Limit: +/- 120 MVAr
- Under-frequency Load Shedding (UFLS) Threshold: 59.2 Hz

Failure to adhere to these limits results in an automated decoupling of the HPM node from the primary Althea backbone to preserve equipment integrity.

4. BATTERY DISCHARGE CYCLE FORECASTING (HPM)
Based on projected load profiles for the High Plains Meridian, the following discharge forecasts are established for the upcoming operational period:

- Peak Evening Ramp: Discharge cycles are forecasted to initiate at 17:30 daily, with a depth of discharge (DoD) reaching 65% by 22:00. 
- Overnight Stabilization: A secondary shallow discharge cycle (approximately 15% DoD) is anticipated between 02:00 and 04:00 to compensate for wind-firming variability.
- Cycle Longevity Forecast: Battery assets within the HPM sector are projected to undergo 1.2 full equivalent cycles per day. Maintenance windows for cell balancing are forecasted for the lowest demand periods on weekend cycles.
