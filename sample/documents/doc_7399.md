# Post-Incident Network Performance Review: Blue Creek Technical Site

**To:** Network Operations Oversight
**From:** Network Operations Center (NOC) Engineering Team
**Date:** October 08, 2025
**Subject:** Performance Correlation Analysis – Incident Date 2025-10-07

## 1. Executive Summary
This report details the infrastructure performance and operational timeline for the Blue Creek Technical Site data center following an identified service degradation on October 7, 2025. This review concentrates on the operational timeline and performance correlations observed during the specified window and does not address external traffic factors or long-term budgetary considerations.

## 2. Infrastructure Overview
The Blue Creek Technical Site serves as a primary regional data center. Its infrastructure is comprised of high-density server rack arrays, redundant core switching fabrics, and multi-pathed fiber interconnects. The site is designed for low-latency throughput, utilizing automated reporting software for continuous performance monitoring and telemetry logging across the terrestrial network layer.

## 3. Incident Timeline and Operational Changes
On 2025-10-07, the NOC Engineering Team initiated a scheduled network protocol change. The following timeline outlines the sequence of events:

*   **07:45 – 08:15:** Deployment of network protocol modifications. This window involved updating routing metrics and protocol distribution across the core site infrastructure.
*   **08:15:** Engineering team identifies a sharp increase in latency metrics within the site's reporting logs. Internal diagnostics confirm that the localized network change was the direct cause of this latency spike.
*   **08:20:** To ensure data integrity for post-incident analysis, the reporting software was successfully synchronized. This synchronization was required to correct and fix log timestamps that had fallen out of alignment during the protocol transition.

## 4. Performance Correlation Findings
An analysis of the reporting logs confirms a direct causal link between the protocol adjustment and the subsequent increase in latency. While the initial intent of the configuration change was to optimize path selection, the immediate reaction involved a degradation of packet transit times within the internal site fabric.

## 5. Future Projections
Following the stabilization of the site at 08:20, the NOC Engineering Team has modeled future performance expectations. Based on the behavior observed during this incident, it is the assertion of this team that future CPU usage at the Blue Creek Technical Site will follow the patterns of the site’s historical network traffic volume. This projection indicates that processor demand will correlate directly with data throughput rather than independent computational load or application processing tasks.

## 6. Closing Statement
The NOC Engineering Team has concluded that the performance fluctuations observed on 2025-10-07 were localized to the Blue Creek infrastructure. No external network traffic data or unrelated site outages were factored into this specific review, as the scope remains limited to the correlation between the protocol update and internal performance logs.
