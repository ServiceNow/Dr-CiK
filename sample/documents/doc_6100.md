RENEWABLE ENERGY RESEARCH LABORATORY
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: IRRADIANCE SENSOR CALIBRATION
Location: Arid Heights Research Annex, The High Plains Meridian, Althea

1. SENSOR POSITIONING AND ALIGNMENT
To ensure accurate recording of Global Horizontal Irradiance, optical sensors must be positioned parallel to the ground. This orientation is required to capture the sum of all direct and diffuse rays striking a flat plane at the Arid Heights Research Annex.

2. SPATIAL CONSTRAINTS
The horizontal orientation of the sensor is a critical calibration factor, as it determines the specific spatial constraint of the measurement. Technicians must verify that the geometric arrangement of the sensor ensures all collected data reflects the total power density hitting a perfectly level, non-tilted area. Failure to maintain a perfectly horizontal plane will result in data that does not accurately represent the irradiance striking the High Plains Meridian site.
