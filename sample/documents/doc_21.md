Marine Research Laboratory - Physical Oceanography Division
Technical Report: Oceanographic Instrument Deployment Report
Subject: WCUR and UCUR Channel Analysis

1. Overview
This technical report details the data retrieved from the Acoustic Doppler Current Profiler (ADCP) deployments at the West Channel (WCUR) and Upper Channel (UCUR) stations. The analysis focuses on the physical and biological signatures recorded during the deployment period, specifically addressing acoustic backscatter, species identification, and velocity harmonics.

2. Analysis of Vertical Backscatter Intensity (WCUR)
Data retrieved from the WCUR deployment reveals distinct variations in acoustic backscatter intensity within the vertical channel. The ADCP signal strength fluctuations indicate clear vertical gradients, likely associated with the concentration of suspended matter and biological organisms. The intensity peaks were observed primarily within the mid-water column, providing a high-resolution profile of the acoustic properties inherent to the WCUR channel. These variations in backscatter serve as a proxy for understanding the density and distribution of volumetric reflectors in the water column.

3. Biological Detection and Planktonic Species
The ADCP beams at both deployment sites successfully detected a diverse range of planktonic species. By analyzing the frequency and strength of the return signals, the following species types were identified within the sampling volume:
- Macrozooplankton (specifically targeting larger crustaceans)
- Small medusae and gelatinous organisms
- Siphonophores
- Micro-planktonic clusters
These biological detections are consistent with the diurnal migration patterns observed in the backscatter data, where the movement of these species contributes significantly to the acoustic profile of the channels.

4. Sound Speed (SSPD) Variability
A summary of the Sound Speed (SSPD) data indicates significant variability throughout the deployment. Analysis shows that these fluctuations are primarily attributed to salinity changes within the localized environment rather than thermal shifts alone. In the UCUR region, influxes of varying salinity levels resulted in measurable deviations in the speed of sound, which required post-processing adjustments to ensure the accuracy of the velocity measurements. The correlation between salinity spikes and SSPD shifts suggests a highly dynamic estuarine or coastal influence within the channels.

5. Tidal Harmonics in UCUR Velocity Data
The velocity data retrieved from the UCUR deployment was subjected to harmonic analysis to determine the influence of tidal constituents. The results confirm the presence of strong tidal harmonics, dominated by the M2 (principal lunar semi-diurnal) and S2 (principal solar semi-diurnal) components. These harmonics are clearly visible in the horizontal velocity vectors, reflecting a predictable periodic oscillation in the Upper Channel. The residual flow, once the tidal harmonics are removed, indicates the underlying non-tidal circulation patterns of the UCUR system.

6. Scope Limitations
This report is strictly limited to the physical and biological data retrieved from the specific WCUR and UCUR ADCP deployments. This document does not include broader regional climate models, operational forecasts for the maritime area, or documentation regarding equipment maintenance logs and instrument service history.
