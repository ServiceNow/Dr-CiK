ALTHEA RENEWABLE ENERGY BUREAU
Technical Monitoring Report: High Plains Meridian Sector

1. SITE OVERVIEW: ZEPHYR PLAINS OBSERVATORY
The Zephyr Plains Observatory is situated within the High Plains Meridian of Althea. Positioned as a premier high-altitude coastal monitoring station, the facility serves as a primary data collection point for solar performance metrics in the region. The unique topography of the High Plains Meridian allows for specialized study of atmospheric solar penetration at elevated coastal altitudes.

2. TECHNICAL DEFINITION: GLOBAL HORIZONTAL IRRADIANCE (GHI)
For the purposes of this technical monitoring report, Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) is defined as the total amount of shortwave radiation received from above by a surface horizontal to the ground. This sun energy is quantified and measured in Watts per squared meter (W/m²). GHI is the sum of direct irradiance and diffuse horizontal irradiance.

3. ESTIMATED SCENARIOS
In high-altitude coastal regions, such as the High Plains Meridian, solar performance is evaluated based on cloudless condition models. Under these idealized scenarios, the expected GHI is calculated to account for reduced atmospheric thickness, though geographical positioning remains the primary factor in determining total potential energy yield.

5. PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS AND LATITUDE VARIANCE
Observations of the recorded peak values (max 412 W/m²) indicate that measurements are significantly lower than standard solar expectations. This discrepancy is not an indication of equipment malfunction, but rather an explanation of latitude variance. The geographic coordinates of the High Plains Meridian result in a solar angle that limits the peak GHI compared to equatorial or lower-latitude monitoring sectors.
