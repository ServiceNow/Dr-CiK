Northern Coastal District Solar Monitoring Station Report

SITE IDENTIFICATION: Zephyr Plains Unit
REPORT TYPE: Technical Operational Report
REPORTING PERIOD: 2022-02-07 to 2022-02-09

1. SITE DESCRIPTION
The Zephyr Plains Observation Post is a specialized technical facility situated within the Northern Coastal District of the Republic of Althea. This monitoring station is strategically positioned to capture ambient solar data unique to the coastal plains, providing critical environmental metrics for the regional power and climate grid.

2. TECHNICAL DEFINITIONS AND BENCHMARKS
Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI): This report utilizes GHI as the primary metric for solar energy assessment. GHI is defined as the total shortwave radiation from the sky falling onto a horizontal surface. It is measured in Watts per square meter (W/m²).

Standard Benchmark Scenario: Under optimal meteorological conditions involving cloudless skies, the horizontal irradiance for this specific location is benchmarked at a peak value of 410 W/m².

3. OBSERVATIONAL ANALYSIS
The Northern Coastal District’s specific latitude significantly influences recorded solar output. Analysis of recent sensor data confirms a trend of lower peak irradiance levels and shorter daylight durations when compared to equatorial benchmarks. These characteristics are directly attributed to the station’s coastal latitude and its resulting solar zenith angles during the February cycle.
