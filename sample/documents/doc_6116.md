1. SITE IDENTIFICATION AND CORE METADATA

1.1 Historical Naming Convention
The facility is formally designated as the "Arid Heights Research Annex." Historically, the site was established under the regional development initiative as “Arid Survey Outpost 04” before being integrated into the current Research Annex framework. In technical correspondence, it is frequently abbreviated as AHRA.

1.2 Geographic Location
The precise geographic coordinates of the Arid Heights Research Annex primary communications mast and sensor array are:
• Latitude: 34° 28' 14.2" N
• Longitude: 116° 02' 55.8" W
• Elevation: 1,142m AMSL

2. INFRASTRUCTURE SPECIFICATIONS

2.1 Horizontal Measurement Platform
The horizontal measurement platform serves as the primary mounting surface for shortwave and longwave radiation sensors. 

• Physical Dimensions: The platform is a rectangular structure measuring 2.5 meters in length by 2.0 meters in width.
• Material Composition: The frame is constructed from powder-coated Grade 6061-T6 aluminum to minimize thermal expansion. The surface plate consists of 5mm thick brushed stainless steel to ensure structural rigidity and corrosion resistance in high-alkalinity environments.

3. INSTRUMENTATION AND PERIPHERAL VARIABLES

This audit confirms the operational status of the secondary sensor suite. The Annex is configured to record a comprehensive set of peripheral meteorological variables simultaneously with core radiation data to provide contextual environmental mapping. These variables include:

• Wind Speed: Monitored via a 3-cup anemometer mounted 3.0m above the platform.
• Atmospheric Pressure: Captured via a digital barometric pressure sensor located within the vented electronics enclosure.
• Ambient Air Temperature
• Relative Humidity
• Precipitation Accumulation

5. SYSTEM SCOPE AND ARCHIVE NOTES

This document is strictly limited to the cataloging of physical infrastructure, metadata standards, and internal data indexing protocols.
