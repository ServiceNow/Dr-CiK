METEOROLOGICAL INSTRUMENTATION DEPARTMENT
TECHNICAL PROCEDURE MANUAL: SOLAR IRRADIANCE MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL

1.0 PHYSICAL DEFINITIONS

Short-wave solar radiation propagates through the atmospheric column as a flux of incoming power. This propagation represents the primary energy input for terrestrial observation at the Arid Heights Research Annex in The High Plains Meridian, The Federative Republic of Althea.

2.0 QUANTIFICATION AND UNITS

The cumulative magnitude of radiant flux is quantified as an intensity value in Watts per square meter (W/m²). This unit of measure provides a standardized metric for assessing the Global Horizontal Irradiance received on a horizontal surface.

3.0 OBSERVATIONAL PARAMETERS

The presence of solar radiation allows for the definition of its physical intensity at a point of observation. Measurement protocols are strictly limited to these physical definitions and standard measurement units.
