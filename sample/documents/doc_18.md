NATIONAL MOORING NETWORK FACILITY
TECHNICAL AND OPERATIONAL REPORT: STATION ALPHA-7
Quarter 3, 2021

I. OVERVIEW
This report documents the maintenance and technical status of the Station Alpha-7 Deep-Sea Assembly for the period of Q3 2021. The focus remains on operational integrity, physical maintenance, and sensor performance during the specified service window.

II. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE (2021-08-20 – 2021-09-14)
Maintenance operations were executed according to the following timeline:
- 2021-08-20: Initial site arrival and structural inspection.
- 2021-08-23: Retrieval of instrument casing and cleaning.
- 2021-08-28: ADCP sensor calibration and health check.
- 2021-09-02: Battery unit testing and power distribution verification.
- 2021-09-08: Satellite link stress-testing and firmware updates.
- 2021-09-14: Final assembly deployment and signal confirmation.

III. ACOUSTIC DOPPLER CURRENT PROFILER (ADCP) CALIBRATION
The ADCP sensor calibration protocols were followed strictly as per manufacturer and facility guidelines. Protocols included:
- Zero-point baseline verification in controlled pressure conditions.
- Transducer sensitivity checks to ensure beam consistency.
- Compass and tilt sensor recalibration to mitigate magnetic deviation.
- Velocity accuracy benchmarking against reference flow meters prior to final deployment.

IV. BATTERY LIFE MONITORING
Continuous monitoring of the assembly’s power supply indicates the following battery life status:
- Primary Power Cell: 84% charge remaining.
- Auxiliary Backup Unit: 98% capacity; verified for emergency failover.
- Integrated components consumption rates are within the anticipated amperage range for deep-sea environments. Voltage output remains stable across both high-draw and idle states.

V. DATA TRANSMISSION AND SATELLITE RELIABILITY
Data transmission rates and satellite link reliability statistics for the National Mooring Network Facility during this period are as follows:
- Average Uplink Throughput: 128 kbps (saturated burst).
- Transmission Success Rate: 99.4%.
- Latency (Round-trip): 720ms.
- Satellite Link Reliability: Minimal packet loss recorded; no unplanned outages during the maintenance window. Data synchronization with the central facility was maintained 24/7.

VI. BIOLOGICAL FOULING CLEANING
Routine cleaning of the instrument housing was performed to combat biological fouling. Documentation of the process is as follows:
- Visual inspection revealed moderate calcareous growth and biofilm accumulation on the primary housing. 
- Non-abrasive mechanical scraping and high-pressure washing were applied to all external surfaces and sensor windows.
- Antifouling treatment was reapplied to the outer casing to inhibit future growth and prevent signal attenuation in the ADCP transducers.

VII. TECHNICAL NOTES
This report is restricted to the maintenance and operational status of Station Alpha-7. It does not contain scientific data analysis, oceanographic research findings, or forecasts regarding future deployment sites.

END OF REPORT
