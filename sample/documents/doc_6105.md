Solar Irradiance Modeling Technical Note: Clear-Sky and Diurnal Constraints

Location: Arid Heights Research Annex, The High Plains Meridian, The Federative Republic of Althea

1. Purpose of Model
This technical note defines the theoretical modeling parameters utilized for Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) calculations. The model calculates theoretical GHI, representing the total amount of sun energy in Watts per squared meter reaching a horizontal surface, assuming an idealized atmosphere without particulate or water vapor obstruction. These modeling parameters provide a clear-sky baseline and do not include broader weather patterns, historical climate data, or forecasts for future energy yields.

2. Diurnal Modeling Constraints
Calculations of total irradiance must account for specific hours when the sun is below the horizon. The lack of direct solar exposure during night intervals results in a modeled potential of zero. Based on the constraints of the three monitored dates, the theoretical potential is zero during the following intervals:
- Pre-dawn: 00:00 to 06:00
- Post-sunset: 18:00 to 21:00

4. Scope Limitation
This source is strictly limited to the theoretical modeling parameters and the specific zero-potential timeframes. The lack of solar exposure during the 00:00–06:00 and 18:00–21:00 blocks results in a modeled potential of zero for these periods.
