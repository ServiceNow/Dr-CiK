METEOROLOGICAL INSTRUMENTATION DEPARTMENT
Standard Operating Procedure: Surface Irradiance Measurement Protocol

1. SCOPE AND OBJECTIVE
This procedure outlines the technical requirements for the quantification of cumulative radiant flux magnitude at the Arid Heights Research Annex. Data is recorded as an intensity value measured in Watts per square meter (W/m²).

2. SENSOR DEPLOYMENT
To ensure accurate data collection, all optical sensors must be positioned perfectly parallel to the ground. This horizontal alignment is a physical necessity; a functional surface or sensor interface is required for the quantification of solar intensity.

3. MEASUREMENT PARAMETERS
The protocol is designed to capture the Global Horizontal Irradiance, defined as the sum of direct and diffuse rays striking a flat plane. This measurement represents the total sun energy arriving on the horizontal surface within the High Plains Meridian.
