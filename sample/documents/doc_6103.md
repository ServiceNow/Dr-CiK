RENEWABLE ENERGY RESEARCH LABORATORY
TECHNICAL REPORT: SOLAR RESOURCE ASSESSMENT DEFINITIONS AND METHODOLOGY

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This technical report outlines the foundational definitions and methodologies employed by the Renewable Energy Research Laboratory in the systematic evaluation of solar resources. The scope of this document is limited strictly to the definition of Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) and its direct utility in geographic solar potential assessment. This report does not include specific coordinate data, site-specific measurements, or broader climate impact forecasts.

2. DEFINITION OF GLOBAL HORIZONTAL IRRADIANCE (GHI)
Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) is defined as the calculated sum of solar energy—representing the total amount of sun energy in Watts per squared meter—arriving on a horizontal surface. For instance, GHI is recorded for locations such as the Arid Heights Research Annex in The High Plains Meridian, The Federative Republic of Althea, to serve as a baseline for regional analysis.

3. METHODOLOGICAL UTILITY
An explanation of Global Horizontal Irradiance is essential as it serves as the primary foundation for assessing the total solar potential at specific coordinates. By isolating the energy received on a horizontal plane, researchers can establish a standardized benchmark for environmental capacity.

Furthermore, the laboratory maintains a formal recognition of Global Horizontal Irradiance as a critical parameter for scientific resource assessment. GHI provides the necessary data framework to compare geographic regions and determine the viability of solar energy harvesting. Without the accurate calculation of this sum, the derived assessment of solar potential would lack the scientific rigor required for technical deployment. This foundational metric ensures that all resource mapping is grounded in measurable radiative values relevant to the specific geographic location under study.
