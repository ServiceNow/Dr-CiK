Subject: December 2021 Solar Irradiance Summary
Report ID: AHRA-SCD-2021-12-W

1. SCOPE AND BACKGROUND
This technical summary details the solar irradiance monitoring conducted at the Arid Heights Research Annex during the December 2021 observation period. The primary focus of this reporting cycle was the 2021 Winter Solstice, a critical window for assessing low-angle solar reception at the facility’s high-altitude coordinates. During this phase, the Annex operated under specific station parameters designed to evaluate sensor responsiveness under minimal atmospheric penetration. 

2. ATMOSPHERIC CALIBRATION AND CLEAR SKY DATA
Throughout the month of December 2021, the Station Calibration Division initiated a series of specialized atmospheric calibration tests. These tests were designed to isolate the impact of stratospheric aerosol density on sensor inputs. As a result of these controlled calibration procedures, data captured during clear sky conditions showed that irradiance values peaked significantly earlier in the day than is typical for this site’s geographical footprint. This temporal shift in peak intensity was a direct byproduct of the calibration methodology applied to the instrumentation arrays during the Solstice window.

3. DECEMBER 2021 SOLAR ENERGY READINGS (CLOUDLESS DAYS)
The following readings represent the verified solar energy throughput recorded on cloudless days during the December 2021 monitoring cycle. These metrics focus on the Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) which measures the total amount of shortwave radiation received from above by a surface horizontal to the ground.

Station Log: December 2021 GHI - Cloudless Day Samples
- Observed Date: 12/05/2021 | Max GHI: 395 W/m²
- Observed Date: 12/12/2021 | Max GHI: 402 W/m²
- Observed Date: 12/18/2021 | Max GHI: 408 W/m²
- Observed Date: 12/21/2021 | Max GHI: 410 W/m²
- Observed Date: 12/28/2021 | Max GHI: 405 W/m²

Based on these observations, the maximum recorded Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) for the month of December 2021 was 410 Watts per squared meter, occurring on the date corresponding with the solar apex of the Winter Solstice period.

4. CONCLUSION
The data collected during this observation cycle confirms that irradiance levels at the Arid Heights Research Annex reached their annual minimum during the Winter Solstice phase of the solar calendar. This reduction in intensity aligns with the seasonal solar trajectory and validates the baseline performance of the station's sensors during the period of lowest astronomical irradiance.

5. OPERATIONAL NOTES AND LIMITATIONS
This report is limited strictly to the 2021 Winter Solstice observation period at the Arid Heights Research Annex. This document does not provide long-term historical comparisons, nor does it offer future energy production forecasts for the facility. Technical specifications and internal schematics of the instrumentation and calibration equipment used during these tests are excluded from this summary and are maintained in a separate technical manual by the Station Calibration Division.
