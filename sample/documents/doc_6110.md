3. SENSOR SENSITIVITY DEGRADATION ANALYSIS
An internal performance analysis was conducted to differentiate between degradation caused by local dust storms and high solar intensity. 

- Dust Storm Impact: High-velocity particulate impacts during local storms lead to physical abrasion of sensor surfaces. Data indicates a 4.2% drop in optical sensitivity directly following the storm event on Feb 5. This is characterized by micro-pitting on the glass interfaces, requiring manual resurfacing.
- Solar Intensity Impact: Conversely, prolonged exposure to high solar intensity resulted in a negligible 0.3% degradation in thermal response. While ultraviolet exposure causes minor discoloration of the sensor casing, the internal electronic sensitivity of the silicon-based components remains stable and within tolerance.

Finding: Local dust storms are the primary driver of physical sensor degradation at AHRA, necessitating more frequent physical intervention than solar-related heat stress.
