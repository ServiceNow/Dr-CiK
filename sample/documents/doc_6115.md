SUBJECT: Monthly Operations and Maintenance – Arid Heights Research Annex (AHRA) and High Plains Meridian (HPM)

1. FACILITY OVERVIEW AND STATUS
This report details the operational logs, personnel arrivals, and technical maintenance performed at the Arid Heights Research Annex and the High Plains Meridian facility for the period of February 1, 2022, through February 28, 2022. Services remained within standard operating parameters for the duration of the month.

2. ARID HEIGHTS RESEARCH ANNEX: INSTRUMENTATION LOGS
The following maintenance activities were recorded for onsite instrumentation:

- Feb 02: Routine calibration check on thermal imaging array.
- Feb 09: Re-leveling of the solar tracking mast following high wind events.
- Feb 14: Inspection of power supply units; no faults detected.
- Feb 18: Reset of the data logging interval timer due to a 4-second drift.
- Feb 24: Connection port cleaning and physical housing inspection for all peripheral sensors.

3. SENSOR MAINTENANCE: CLEANING SCHEDULE
Specific attention was given to horizontal sensor surfaces to ensure data integrity. Due to local dust accumulation, the following cleaning schedule was executed:

- Weekly (Feb 4, 11, 18, 25): Standard dry-brushing of all horizontal surfaces.
- Bi-Weekly (Feb 10, 24): Wet-wipe cleaning using specialized optical solution on irradiance sensor apertures to remove fine particulate matter.
- Ad-hoc (Feb 15): Emergency clearing of wind-blown debris from the primary sensor platform.

4. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
- Irradiance Sensor Firmware: All active irradiance units are currently operating on Firmware Version 4.2.1 (Build 88).
- Site Data Transmission: All automated data transmission between the site and the central hub is conducted via a dedicated LTE-M wireless protocol, utilizing a secure VPN tunnel.
