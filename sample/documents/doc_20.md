### Operations and Maintenance Manual: Regional Maritime Monitoring Network

**Issuer:** National Oceanographic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) / Integrated Ocean Observing System (IOOS)
**Document Class:** Technical and Operational Handbook

---

#### 1. Sensor Specifications: Sea Water Temperature (TEMP)
To ensure high-fidelity longitudinal data, all deployed sea water temperature (TEMP) sensors must adhere to standard resolution parameters. The monitoring network requires a minimum sensor resolution of 0.001°C to capture subtle thermodynamic changes in the maritime environment. Accuracy must be maintained through bi-annual calibration cycles to prevent data drift in the regional nodes.

#### 2. Data Management and Archival Protocols
The Regional Maritime Monitoring Network operates on a standardized 1-hour interval for primary data acquisition. Software protocols for processing this data are as follows:

*   **Processing Software:** Data must be processed through the IOOS-compliant Quality Assurance of Real-Time Oceanographic Data (QARTOD) software suite.
*   **Intervals:** Raw samples are aggregated and averaged into 1-hour discrete intervals.
*   **Archival Formats:** All processed 1-hour interval data sets must be archived in NetCDF (Network Common Data Form) or CSV format, ensuring metadata headers align with the Climate and Forecast (CF) conventions for interoperability with the National Centers for Environmental Information (NCEI).

#### 3. Administrative and Funding Sources
Operational sustainability of the monitoring zones is supported through distinct administrative funding sources. These allocations are distributed as follows:

*   **Coastal Zone Alpha:** Funded via the NOAA Integrated Ocean Observing System (IOOS) regional grant allocation.
*   **Deep-Water Transition Zones:** Primarily supported by the National Science Foundation (NSF) under the Oceanic Research Initiative.
*   **Estuarine Monitoring Nodes:** Administrative oversight and funding provided through Federal-State Cooperative Agreements and the Office of Coastal Management (OCM).

#### 4. Operational Safety: Deep-Sea Assembly (DSA) Servicing
Safety remains the primary objective during the maintenance of the Deep-Sea Assembly (DSA). Vessel-based crews must adhere to the following safety protocols during servicing operations:

*   **Pre-Deployment Briefing:** A mandatory safety meeting for all vessel-based personnel must occur at least 1 hour prior to reaching the DSA site.
*   **Handling Procedures:** Utilizing cranes or winches for the recovery of the Deep-Sea Assembly requires a certified rigger. All crew members must wear Level III Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), including steel-toed boots and hard hats, during heavy lifting maneuvers.
*   **Lock-out/Tag-out:** Before any electronic servicing of the DSA begins, all power sources must be physically locked out and tagged to prevent accidental energization.
*   **Vessel Stability:** Operations must be suspended if sea state conditions exceed Beaufort Scale 6 or if the Vessel Master determines the deck environment is unsafe for hardware retrieval.

#### 5. Exclusions
This manual is strictly limited to technical specifications, data management procedures, and operational safety. This document does not provide historical climate data, upcoming weather forecasts, or specific sensor procurement invoices. For financial records or meteorological predictions, please refer to the respective administrative or forecasting divisions.
